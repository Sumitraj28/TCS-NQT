# Para Jumbles — Complete Notes

---

## 1. Finding the Opening Sentence

The opening sentence of a jumbled paragraph must be independent and introduce the main topic.

### Characteristics of an Opening Sentence
*   It does **not** start with transition words like "however," "therefore," "but," "moreover," or "thus."
*   It does **not** contain pronouns referencing previously mentioned entities (e.g., "he," "she," "it," "they," or "this plan") unless the entity is defined within the same sentence.
*   It is typically a general definition, statement of fact, or introduction of a concept/name.

---

## 2. Mandatory Pairs (Transition & Pronoun Clues)

The key to solving Para Jumbles quickly is not trying to find the entire sequence at once. Instead, identify **mandatory pairs** (two sentences that must follow one after the other) and look for options containing that pair.

### A. Pronoun Reference Links (Noun-Pronoun)
A noun must be introduced before a pronoun can reference it.
*   *Sentence A:* **Albert Einstein** was a theoretical physicist.
*   *Sentence B:* **He** developed the theory of relativity.
*   *Link:* Sentence A must come before Sentence B ($A \rightarrow B$).

### B. Transition Words (Conjunction Links)
Transition words reveal the logical relation between consecutive sentences:
*   *Addition/Sequence:* "Furthermore," "In addition," "Firstly," "Secondly" $\rightarrow$ continue the same direction.
*   *Contrast/Shift:* "However," "On the other hand," "Nevertheless" $\rightarrow$ show a change in tone or direction.
*   *Conclusion/Result:* "Therefore," "Consequently," "Thus" $\rightarrow$ indicate the final sentence or result of an argument.

### C. Acronym / Full-Name Rule
The full name or complete term is introduced before using its short form or acronym.
*   *Incorrect:* ISRO was formed in 1969. The Indian Space Research Organisation is based in Bengaluru.
*   *Correct Order:* Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) $\rightarrow$ ISRO.

---

## 3. Finding the Closing Sentence

The closing sentence summarizes the argument or states the final consequence.
*   **Keywords:** Look for sentences starting with "Thus," "Consequently," "Therefore," "In conclusion," or "Finally."
*   **Content:** It represents a wrap-up or future outlook rather than introducing new details.
