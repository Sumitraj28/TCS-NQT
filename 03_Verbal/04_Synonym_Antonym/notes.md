# Synonym & Antonym — Complete Notes

---

## 1. Contextual Word Meaning

In many TCS NQT questions, a vocabulary word is presented inside a sentence. Finding its synonym or antonym depends on analyzing the context.

*   **Rule:** Do not jump to the most common definition of the word. A word can have multiple meanings based on its part of speech and surrounding words.
*   *Example:* "The company experienced **volatile** stock prices."
    *   *Volatile* in chemistry means "evaporating rapidly."
    *   *Volatile* in finance means "unstable" or "subject to rapid change."
    *   Therefore, the correct synonym is **unstable**, not *gaseous*.

## 2. Root-Word Clues & Affixes

When you encounter an unfamiliar word, break it down into its constituent morphological parts: Prefix + Root + Suffix.

### A. Root Words
Many English words are derived from Latin or Greek roots:
*   **Bene** (good/well): *Benevolent*, *beneficial*.
*   **Mal** (bad/evil): *Malevolent*, *malicious*, *malignant*.
*   **Chron** (time): *Chronological*, *chronic*, *synchronize*.
*   **Cred** (believe): *Credible*, *credulous*, *incredible*.

### B. Prefixes (Tonal Shifters)
Prefixes change the tone or direction of a word:
*   *Negative Prefixes:* **un-**, **in-**, **im-**, **dis-**, **a-** (e.g., *atypical*, *disingenuous*, *implausible*).
*   *Directional Prefixes:* **sub-** (under), **super-** (above), **inter-** (between), **intra-** (within).

### C. Suffixes (Part of Speech Indicators)
Suffixes do not change the core meaning but indicate grammatical category:
*   *Noun:* **-tion**, **-ness**, **-ity**, **-ment**.
*   *Adjective:* **-ous**, **-ful**, **-able**, **-ive**.
*   *Verb:* **-ize**, **-ify**, **-ate**.

---

## 3. Option Elimination Strategies

1.  **Charge (Tone) Check:** Determine if the word has a positive, negative, or neutral tone. If the question asks for a synonym of a positive word, eliminate options with negative tones.
2.  **Part of Speech Match:** The synonym or antonym of a verb is always a verb; for an adjective, it is an adjective.
3.  **Antonym-Synonym Options Trap:** Examiners often include the *antonym* of the word as an option when asking for a *synonym* (and vice versa) to catch careless readers. Always double-check whether the question asks for the Synonym or the Antonym.
