# Direct & Indirect Speech — Complete Notes

---

## 1. Core Conversion Rules

Direct speech repeats the exact words spoken. Indirect (reported) speech reports the statement from a third-person perspective.

### Shift Framework (When the Reporting Verb is in the Past Tense)
If the reporting verb is in the past tense (e.g., *said*, *told*), we must shift the tense and time markers in the reported clause.

#### A. Pronoun Shifts (SON Rule)
*   **S**ubject (First person pronouns: *I, we*) changes according to the **Subject** of the reporting verb.
*   **O**bject (Second person pronouns: *you*) changes according to the **Object** of the reporting verb.
*   **N**o change for Third person pronouns (*he, she, it, they*).

#### B. Time and Place Marker Shifts
*   *this* $\rightarrow$ *that*
*   *these* $\rightarrow$ *those*
*   *here* $\rightarrow$ *there*
*   *now* $\rightarrow$ *then*
*   *today* $\rightarrow$ *that day*
*   *yesterday* $\rightarrow$ *the day before* or *the previous day*
*   *tomorrow* $\rightarrow$ *the next day* or *the following day*
*   *ago* $\rightarrow$ *before*

## 2. Tense Transformation Table

Below is the reference table showing how tenses shift back in time during reporting.

| Direct Tense | Indirect Tense | Direct Example | Indirect Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Simple Present** | Simple Past | She said, "I **write** a letter." | She said that she **wrote** a letter. |
| **Present Continuous** | Past Continuous | She said, "I **am writing** a letter." | She said that she **was writing** a letter. |
| **Present Perfect** | Past Perfect | She said, "I **have written** a letter." | She said that she **had written** a letter. |
| **Simple Past** | Past Perfect | She said, "I **wrote** a letter." | She said that she **had written** a letter. |
| **Past Continuous** | Past Perfect Continuous | She said, "I **was writing** a letter." | She said that she **had been writing** a letter. |
| **Simple Future (will)** | Conditional (would) | She said, "I **will write** a letter." | She said that she **would write** a letter. |

*Note: If the reporting verb is in the present or future tense (e.g., "She says" or "She will say"), the tense of the reported clause does not change.*

## 3. Interrogative & Imperative Conversions

### A. Interrogative Sentences (Questions)
*   **Yes/No Questions:** Remove "said to" and replace with "asked". Use **if** or **whether** to connect clauses.
    *   *Direct:* He said to me, "Are you coming?"
    *   *Indirect:* He **asked** me **if** I **was coming**.
*   **Wh- Questions:** Do not use "if/whether". The question word (*who, what, where, why*) acts as the connector.
    *   *Direct:* He said to me, "Where are you going?"
    *   *Indirect:* He **asked** me **where** I **was going**.

### B. Imperative Sentences (Orders, Requests)
*   Replace "said to" with *ordered, requested, advised, warned, or forbade*. Connect the reported clause using an infinitive (**to + V1**).
    *   *Direct:* The doctor said, "Take this medicine."
    *   *Indirect:* The doctor **advised** me **to take** that medicine.
