# Error Detection — Complete Notes

---

## 1. Subject-Verb Agreement

A verb must agree in number (singular or plural) with its true subject.

### Key Rules & Flagged Patterns
*   **Intervening Phrases:** Do not let phrases starting with "along with," "as well as," "together with," or "in addition to" confuse you about the subject's number.
    *   *Incorrect:* The captain, along with his crew members, **were** praised.
    *   *Correct:* The captain, along with his crew members, **was** praised (Subject is singular: *captain*).
*   **Either/Or and Neither/Nor:** When subjects are joined by "either... or" or "neither... nor", the verb agrees with the **closest** subject.
    *   *Incorrect:* Neither the manager nor the employees **was** present.
    *   *Correct:* Neither the manager nor the employees **were** present (Closest subject is plural: *employees*).
*   **Collective Nouns:** Nouns like "committee," "jury," or "team" take a singular verb when acting as a unified unit, but a plural verb if the members are acting individually.
    *   *Incorrect:* The jury **were** unanimous in its decision.
    *   *Correct:* The jury **was** unanimous in its decision.

## 2. Tenses

Verbs must maintain logical tense consistency throughout a sentence or paragraph.

### Key Rules & Flagged Patterns
*   **Past Tense Main Clause:** If the main clause is in the past tense, subordinate clauses must also be in a past tense (unless expressing a universal truth).
    *   *Incorrect:* He told me that he **is** coming tomorrow.
    *   *Correct:* He told me that he **was** coming the next day.
*   **Since/For with Perfect Tenses:** When expressing an action that started in the past and is still continuing, use the Present Perfect Continuous tense, not the Simple Present or Present Continuous.
    *   *Incorrect:* I am living in Delhi **since** five years.
    *   *Correct:* I have been living in Delhi **for** five years.

## 3. Prepositions

Using the wrong preposition or adding unnecessary prepositions is a high-frequency error type in TCS NQT.

### Key Rules & Flagged Patterns
*   **Standard Collocations (Fixed Prepositions):**
    *   *Abide **by*** (rules)
    *   *Accused **of*** (crime) - *not accused with*
    *   *Angry **with** a person* vs. *Angry **at** a situation*
    *   *Congratulate **on*** (success) - *not congratulate for*
*   **Redundant Prepositions:** Some verbs should not be followed by a preposition.
    *   *Incorrect:* He discussed **about** the matter. / She entered **into** the room.
    *   *Correct:* He discussed the matter. / She entered the room.

---

## 4. Articles

Errors in using indefinite articles ("a," "an") and definite articles ("the").

### Key Rules & Flagged Patterns
*   **Vowel Sound vs. Vowel Letter:** "An" is used before vowel **sounds**, not letters.
    *   *Incorrect:* He is **a** honest man. / He is **an** university student.
    *   *Correct:* He is **an** honest man (silent 'h' -> vowel sound). / He is **a** university student ("yu" consonant sound).
*   **Unique Geographical Features:** Use "the" before oceans, rivers, mountain ranges, and countries with collective names (e.g., USA, UK).
    *   *Incorrect:* He wants to cross **the** Mount Everest. (Do not use "the" before individual mountain peaks).
    *   *Correct:* He wants to cross Mount Everest.
    *   *Incorrect:* He sailed across **Pacific Ocean**.
    *   *Correct:* He sailed across **the** Pacific Ocean.
