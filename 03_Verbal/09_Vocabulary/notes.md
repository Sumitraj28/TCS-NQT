# High-Frequency Vocabulary List (100 Words)

This is a curated list of 100 high-frequency words tested in the TCS NQT verbal section. Each entry includes the word, part of speech, meaning, a synonym/antonym pair, and a contextual example sentence.

---

### 1. Aberration (Noun)
*   **Meaning:** A departure from what is normal, usual, or expected.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Deviation / Normality
*   **Usage:** The sudden system crash was a temporary software aberration.

### 2. Abstain (Verb)
*   **Meaning:** Restrain oneself from doing or enjoying something.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Refrain / Indulge
*   **Usage:** He chose to abstain from voting on the controversial proposal.

### 3. Acumen (Noun)
*   **Meaning:** The ability to make good judgments and take quick decisions.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Shrewdness / Ignorance
*   **Usage:** Her business acumen helped the startup secure its first major funding.

### 4. Admonish (Verb)
*   **Meaning:** Warn or reprimand someone firmly.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Scold / Praise
*   **Usage:** The manager admonished the team for failing to meet the deadline.

### 5. Alacrity (Noun)
*   **Meaning:** Brisk and cheerful readiness.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Eagerness / Apathy
*   **Usage:** The intern accepted the difficult assignment with alacrity.

### 6. Ameliorate (Verb)
*   **Meaning:** Make something bad better.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Improve / Worsen
*   **Usage:** The new policy was designed to ameliorate working conditions in the factory.

### 7. Anomaly (Noun)
*   **Meaning:** Something that deviates from what is standard, normal, or expected.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Oddity / Regularity
*   **Usage:** A sudden spike in the sensor data was flagged as an anomaly.

### 8. Antipathy (Noun)
*   **Meaning:** A deep-seated feeling of dislike.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Hostility / Affection
*   **Usage:** His natural antipathy toward public speaking made presentations stressful.

### 9. Apathy (Noun)
*   **Meaning:** Lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Indifference / Enthusiasm
*   **Usage:** Voter apathy was blamed for the low turnout during the elections.

### 10. Ascetic (Noun/Adj)
*   **Meaning:** Practicing strict self-denial for spiritual discipline.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Austere / Sybaritic
*   **Usage:** The monk lived an ascetic life in a remote mountain cave.

### 11. Assuage (Verb)
*   **Meaning:** Make an unpleasant feeling less intense.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Relieve / Aggravate
*   **Usage:** The CEO's reassurance helped to assuage the fears of the employees.

### 12. Audacious (Adj)
*   **Meaning:** Showing a willingness to take surprisingly bold risks.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Bold / Timid
*   **Usage:** The team launched an audacious plan to conquer the international market.

### 13. Austere (Adj)
*   **Meaning:** Severe or strict in manner, attitude, or appearance.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Plain / Luxurious
*   **Usage:** The architect designed an austere building with zero decorative elements.

### 14. Banal (Adj)
*   **Meaning:** So lacking in originality as to be obvious and boring.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Trite / Creative
*   **Usage:** The movie had a banal plot that we had seen dozens of times before.

### 15. Belligerent (Adj)
*   **Meaning:** Hostile and aggressive.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Combative / Peaceful
*   **Usage:** The customer became belligerent when his request was denied.

### 16. Benevolent (Adj)
*   **Meaning:** Well-meaning and kindly.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Charitable / Malevolent
*   **Usage:** The benevolent donor funded a new library for the community.

### 17. Cacophony (Noun)
*   **Meaning:** A harsh, discordant mixture of sounds.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Noise / Harmony
*   **Usage:** The market was filled with a loud cacophony of vendors and traffic.

### 18. Capricious (Adj)
*   **Meaning:** Given to sudden and unaccountable changes of mood or behavior.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Fickle / Constant
*   **Usage:** The capricious weather ruined our plans for an outdoor picnic.

### 19. Castigate (Verb)
*   **Meaning:** Reprimand someone severely.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Chastise / Commend
*   **Usage:** The board castigated the director for mismanagement of funds.

### 20. Catalyst (Noun)
*   **Meaning:** A substance or person that increases the rate of a reaction or event.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Stimulant / Inhibitor
*   **Usage:** The new invention acted as a catalyst for economic growth in the region.

### 21. Chicanery (Noun)
*   **Meaning:** The use of trickery to achieve a financial or political purpose.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Deception / Honesty
*   **Usage:** The lawyer was suspended for engaging in legal chicanery.

### 22. Cogent (Adj)
*   **Meaning:** Clear, logical, and convincing.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Compelling / Weak
*   **Usage:** She presented a cogent argument in favor of upgrading the systems.

### 23. Condone (Verb)
*   **Meaning:** Accept and allow behavior that is considered morally wrong to continue.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Excuse / Condemn
*   **Usage:** The university does not condone plagiarism under any circumstances.

### 24. Conundrum (Noun)
*   **Meaning:** A confusing and difficult problem or question.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Puzzle / Solution
*   **Usage:** Finding a balance between cost and quality is a constant conundrum.

### 25. Corroborate (Verb)
*   **Meaning:** Confirm or give support to a statement or theory.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Verify / Contradict
*   **Usage:** The security footage helped to corroborate the witness's statement.

### 26. Deference (Noun)
*   **Meaning:** Humble submission and respect.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Respect / Disrespect
*   **Usage:** He addressed the judge with great deference.

### 27. Delineate (Verb)
*   **Meaning:** Describe or portray something precisely.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Outline / Confuse
*   **Usage:** The project charter clearly delineates the roles of each member.

### 28. Deride (Verb)
*   **Meaning:** Express contempt for; ridicule.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Mock / Respect
*   **Usage:** Critics derided the new art exhibition as simplistic and unoriginal.

### 29. Desiccated (Adj)
*   **Meaning:** Lacking interest, passion, or energy; dried out.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Dehydrated / Moist
*   **Usage:** The researcher found desiccated plant specimens in the ancient tomb.

### 30. Desultory (Adj)

*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Half-hearted / Systematic
*   **Usage:** He made a desultory attempt to study for the test, flipping pages lazily.

### 31. Diatribe (Noun)

*   **Usage:** The politician launched a bitter diatribe against his opponents.

### 32. Diffident (Adj)
*   **Meaning:** Modest or shy because of a lack of self-confidence.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Hesitant / Confident
*   **Usage:** The diffident boy stood at the back of the classroom, avoiding eye contact.

### 33. Dilatory (Adj)
*   **Meaning:** Slow to act; intended to cause delay.
*   **Synonym/Antonym:** Delaying / Prompt

### 34. Diligent (Adj)

### 35. Disparate (Adj)

### 37. Dogmatic (Adj)

### 40. Efficacy (Noun)

### 41. Eloquent (Adj)

### 42. Embellish (Verb)

*   **Usage:** He chose to embellish the story with exaggerated details.

### 43. Emulate (Verb)

### 44. Enervate (Verb)

### 45. Ephemeral (Adj)
