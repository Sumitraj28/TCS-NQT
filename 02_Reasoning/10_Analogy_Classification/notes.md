## 1. Analogy (Word & Number relationships)

An **analogy** is a comparison between two things that are similar in some way. The question format is typically:

$$A : B :: C : ?$$

This means "A is related to B in the same way that C is related to the missing term."

### A. Word Analogies
Word analogies test vocabulary and general knowledge relationships.
*   **Common Relationship Types:**
    1.  *Country and Capital:* India : New Delhi :: France : Paris.
    2.  *Synonyms/Antonyms:* Huge : Giant :: Tiny : Micro.
    3.  *Worker and Tool:* Writer : Pen :: Chef : Knife.
    4.  *Product and Raw Material:* Bread : Flour :: Jewelry : Gold.

### B. Number Analogies
These test mathematical relationships.
*   **Common Relationships:**
    1.  *Square/Cube:* $4 : 64 :: 5 : 125$ ($x : x^3$).
    2.  *Multiplication + Addition:* $6 : 15 :: 8 : 19$ ($x : 2x + 3$).
    3.  *Prime Succession:* $7 : 11 :: 13 : 17$ (Consecutive primes).

## 2. Classification (Odd-One-Out)

Classification tests your ability to identify the odd element out of a group that does not share a common characteristic.

### A. Verbal Classification (Odd-One-Out)
Identify the word that doesn't belong to the semantic class.
*   *Example:* Apple, Mango, Potato, Orange.
    *   Potato is the odd one out because it is a vegetable (and grows underground), while others are fruits.

### B. Numerical Classification
Identify the number that does not follow the mathematical rule of the group.
*   **Key Filters to Check (in order):**
    1.  *Prime Numbers:* Are three numbers prime and one composite?
    2.  *Squares/Cubes:* Are three numbers perfect squares/cubes?
    3.  *Divisibility:* Are three numbers divisible by a certain prime (e.g., 7 or 11) while the fourth is not?
    4.  *Digit Sum:* Do the digit sums of three numbers equal a constant value?
        *   *Example:* 125, 341, 512, 233.
            *   $1+2+5=8$, $3+4+1=8$, $5+1+2=8$, but $2+3+3=8$ too. Let's look for another rule.
            *   $125 = 5^3$, $512 = 8^3$, but 341 and 233 are not cubes.
            *   Let's check odd/even or prime status.
