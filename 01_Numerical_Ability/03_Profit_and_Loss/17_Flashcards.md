# Profit & Loss Flashcards

Use these cards for active recall and spaced repetition practice.

| # | Front (Question) | Back (Answer) | Memory Trick |
|---|-----------------|---------------|--------------|
| 1 | What is the base reference (100%) for Profit/Loss %? | Cost Price (CP). | CP is your actual cash investment. |
| 2 | What is the base reference (100%) for Discount %? | Marked Price (MP). | MP is the printed label being discounted. |
| 3 | What is the formula for successive discounts $d_1$ and $d_2$? | $$d_{\text{net}} = d_1 + d_2 - \frac{d_1 d_2}{100}\%$$ | The negative term corrects the shifting base. |
| 4 | What is the profit formula for a dishonest dealer cheating on weight? | $$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{\text{Error}}{\text{Delivered}} \times 100\%$$ | Delivered weight is the dealer's investment. |
| 5 | What is the discount percentage for a "Buy X, get Y free" deal? | $$\text{Discount}\% = \frac{Y}{X+Y} \times 100\%$$ | Total bundle count goes in denominator. |
| 6 | If CP of X = SP of Y, what is the CP-SP ratio? | $$\frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{X}{Y}$$ | Cross-multiply terms: $X \times CP = Y \times SP$. |
| 7 | What is the net result of selling two items at same SP, one at $x\%$ profit, one at $x\%$ loss? | Net Loss $= \left(\frac{x}{10}\right)^2\%$ | This transaction always leads to a loss. |
| 8 | What formula links Marked Price (MP) and Cost Price (CP) directly? | $$\frac{MP}{CP} = \frac{100 + P\%}{100 - D\%}$$ | Profit raises MP, discount lowers CP base. |
| 9 | What is the definition of Markup %? | $$\text{Markup}\% = \frac{MP - CP}{CP} \times 100$$ | Markup is calculated on CP base. |
| 10| How do you calculate CP from SP when profit $P\%$ is given? | $$CP = \frac{SP}{1 + P/100}$$ | Divide SP by multiplier (do NOT multiply). |
| 11| If a dealer cheats by $10\%$ in both buying and selling, what is his net profit%? | $$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{10 + 10}{100 - 10} \times 100 = 22.22\%$$ | Formula: $\frac{\%B + \%S}{100 - \%S} \times 100$. |
| 12| How do overheads like transport/repairs affect CP? | They are added directly to CP. | Total CP = Purchase Price + Overheads. |
| 13| What is the net profit% for a $20\%$ markup followed by a $10\%$ discount? | $$8\%\text{ Profit}$$ | Apply successive change: $20 - 10 - 2 = 8\%$. |
| 14| What is the net profit% for a $30\%$ markup followed by a $30\%$ discount? | $$9\%\text{ Loss}$$ | Symmetric markup-discount is always loss: $3^2\% = 9\%$. |
| 15| In mixture alligation, can you use the mixture's Selling Price directly? | No, convert mixture SP to mixture CP first. | Mean CP $= \frac{SP}{1 + P/100}$. |
| 16| How should you enter numerical answers on the NQT exam interface? | Enter numbers only (e.g. `20` instead of `20%`). | Avoid units, symbols, or letters. |
| 17| What does cheating by $10\%$ when buying mean? | Getting $1100$g for the price of $1000$g. | Volume multiplier $= 1.10$. |
| 18| What does cheating by $10\%$ when selling mean? | Delivering $900$g while charging for $1000$g. | Volume multiplier $= \frac{10}{9}$. |
| 19| If a $20\%$ price reduction allows buying 4kg more for ₹160, what is the reduced price? | $$₹8/\text{kg}$$ | Reduced Price $= \frac{20\% \text{ of } 160}{4} = \frac{32}{4} = 8$. |
| 20| If a merchant makes a profit of $25\%$ on SP, what is his profit on CP? | $$33.33\%$$ | SP $= 100 \implies$ Profit $= 25 \implies CP = 75 \implies \frac{25}{75} = 33.33\%$. |
