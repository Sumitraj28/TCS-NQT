# Profit & Loss Revision Guide

---

### ⚡ 1-Minute Revision
*   **Base reference**: Profit/Loss percentage is calculated on Cost Price (CP). Discount percentage is calculated on Marked Price (MP).
*   **Successive discounts**: Combined discount is $d_1 + d_2 - \frac{d_1 d_2}{100}\%$ (never add them linearly).
*   **Article count**: If CP of X = SP of Y $\implies \frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{X}{Y}$.
*   **Dishonest dealer**: Profit is based on delivered weight: $\frac{\text{Error}}{\text{Delivered}} \times 100\%$.
*   **Equal SP**: If two items are sold at same price, one at $x\%$ profit and one at $x\%$ loss, net loss is always $(\frac{x}{10})^2\%$.

---

### ⏱ 5-Minute Revision
Review these core formulas and examples:

1.  **Successive Markup-Discount**:
    $$\text{Profit}\% = m - d - \frac{md}{100}\%$$
    *   *Example*: $+20\%$ markup, $-10\%$ discount $\implies 20 - 10 - 2 = 8\%$ Profit.
2.  **Dishonest Dealer**:
    $$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{\text{Error}}{\text{Delivered}} \times 100\%$$
    *   *Example*: Delivers 950g instead of 1kg $\implies \frac{50}{950} \times 100 = 5.26\%$.
3.  **Buy X, Get Y Free**:
    $$\text{Discount}\% = \frac{Y}{X+Y} \times 100\%$$
    *   *Example*: Buy 4, get 1 free $\implies \frac{1}{5} \times 100 = 20\%$ discount.
4.  **Symmetric Equal SP Loss**:
    $$\text{Loss}\% = \left(\frac{x}{10}\right)^2\%$$
    *   *Example*: Same SP, $+15\%$ and $-15\% \implies 1.5^2 = 2.25\%$ Net Loss.

### 📖 15-Minute Revision
Review this conceptual recap and attempt the 3 questions.

#### Concept Recap:
Profit & Loss is the study of relative percentage changes on shifting base variables. Cost Price (CP) is the base for markups and profit/losses. Marked Price (MP) is the base for discounts. When dealing with dishonest dealer problems, remember that the dealer's actual investment is the weight he delivers (delivered weight). For multiple transaction chains, represent each step as a multiplying factor fraction and chain them to find the initial or final values.

#### Quick Test Questions:

**Q1.** By selling an item for ₹540, a man loses $10\%$. What should be the Selling Price to gain $10\%$?
*   *Solution*: $90\% \text{ of CP} = 540 \implies CP = 600$. New SP $= 600 \times 1.10 = ₹660$.

**Q2.** The CP of 12 pencils equals the SP of 9 pencils. Find the profit percentage.
*   *Solution*: $\frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{12}{9} = \frac{4}{3} \implies \text{Profit} = 33.33\%$.

**Q3.** Find the single discount equivalent to successive discounts of $20\%$ and $5\%$.
*   *Solution*: $20 + 5 - \frac{100}{100} = 25 - 1 = 24\%$ discount.

### 🌙 Night-Before-Exam Revision
*   **Formula Checks**: Memorize the MP/CP ratio link: $\frac{MP}{CP} = \frac{100+P}{100-D}$.
*   **Dishonest Dealer Check**: Verify that delivered weight is in the denominator.
*   **Top 3 Traps to Avoid**:
    1.  Adding successive discounts directly (e.g. $10\% + 10\% \neq 20\%$).
    2.  Applying discounts to Cost Price instead of Marked Price.
    3.  Typing currency symbols or percent signs into FIB answer boxes on the exam screen.

### 🏃 Exam-Hall Revision (30 seconds)
Use this single comparison table for rapid reference right before entering the exam:

| Spotted Phrasing | Mathematical Relation | Shortcut |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **"CP of X = SP of Y"** | $X \times CP = Y \times SP$ | Profit/Loss% $= \frac{X-Y}{Y} \times 100$ |
| **"Buy X, get Y free"** | $\text{Discount} = \frac{Y}{X+Y}$ | Divide free items by total bundle count |
| **"Cheats on weight by X grams"** | $\text{Profit} = \frac{\text{Error}}{\text{Delivered}}$ | Delivered weight goes in denominator |
| **"Same SP, +x% and -x%"** | Net Loss $= (\frac{x}{10})^2\%$ | Always a net loss; CP values differ |
| **"Markup of m% and discount of d%"**| Net $= m - d - \frac{md}{100}$ | Use positive/negative signs carefully |
