# Profit & Loss Shortcuts

Use these 5 speed hacks to solve Profit & Loss questions in under 30 seconds during the NQT.

---

## 1. The CP-SP Ratio Trick
*   **When to Use**: Questions stating "the cost price of $X$ articles equals the selling price of $Y$ articles."
*   **How it Works**: 
    1.  Write the equation: $X \times CP = Y \times SP$.
    2.  Form the ratio:
        $$\frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{X}{Y}$$
    3.  Compare $X$ and $Y$ to find Profit or Loss:
        $$\text{Change}\% = \left(\frac{X - Y}{Y}\right) \times 100$$
*   **Worked Example**: If the CP of 15 pens equals the SP of 12 pens, find the profit percentage.
    *   Equation: $15 \times CP = 12 \times SP \implies \frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{15}{12} = \frac{5}{4}$.
    *   Calculation: Since the ratio is $\frac{5}{4}$, the profit is $\frac{1}{4} = 25\%$.
*   **Time Saved**: 30 seconds (avoids assuming dummy variables or algebraic constants).

---

## 2. Dishonest Dealer Weights Shortcut
*   **When to Use**: A shopkeeper sells goods at cost price but uses a lighter weight.
*   **How it Works**: Calculate profit directly using the ratio of error to actual weight delivered:
    $$\text{Profit}\% = \left(\frac{\text{Weight Cheated}}{\text{Weight Delivered}}\right) \times 100 = \left(\frac{\text{Error}}{\text{True Weight} - \text{Error}}\right) \times 100$$
*   **Worked Example**: A shopkeeper sells sugar at cost price but uses a weight of 800 grams instead of 1 kilogram. Find his profit percentage.
    *   Weight Cheated (Error) $= 1000 - 800 = 200\text{g}$.
    *   Weight Delivered $= 800\text{g}$.
    *   Calculation:
        $$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{200}{800} \times 100 = \frac{1}{4} \times 100 = 25\%$$
*   **Time Saved**: 45 seconds (bypasses calculating absolute prices for weights).

---

## 3. "Buy X, Get Y Free" Discount Trick
*   **When to Use**: Questions showing promotional buy-get offers (e.g. "Buy 3, get 1 free").
*   **How it Works**: Convert the bundle directly into a discount percentage:
    $$\text{Discount}\% = \left(\frac{\text{Free Items (Y)}}{\text{Total Items Received (X + Y)}}\right) \times 100$$
*   **Worked Example**: A shop offers a "Buy 5, get 3 free" deal. What is the equivalent discount percentage?
    *   Free Items $= 3$.
    *   Total Items $= 5 + 3 = 8$.
    *   Calculation:
        $$\text{Discount}\% = \frac{3}{8} \times 100 = 37.5\%$$
*   **Time Saved**: 20 seconds.

---

## 4. The Multiplying Factor (MF) Chain
*   **When to Use**: Successive transactions where an item passes through multiple people ($A \rightarrow B \rightarrow C \rightarrow D$).
*   **How it Works**: Represent profit as a multiplier $(1 + P/100)$ and loss as $(1 - L/100)$. Multiply them sequentially:
    $$CP_{\text{Final}} = CP_{\text{Initial}} \times MF_A \times MF_B \times MF_C$$
*   **Worked Example**: A sells a watch to B at $20\%$ profit, and B sells it to C at a $10\%$ loss. If C pays ₹1,080, find A's cost price.
    *   $MF_A = 1.20 = \frac{6}{5}$.
    *   $MF_B = 0.90 = \frac{9}{10}$.
    *   Equation:
        $$CP_A \times \frac{6}{5} \times \frac{9}{10} = 1080 \implies CP_A \times \frac{54}{50} = 1080$$
    *   Calculation:
        $$CP_A = 1080 \times \frac{50}{54} = 20 \times 50 = ₹1,000$$
*   **Time Saved**: 40 seconds (avoids solving intermediate selling prices).

---

## 5. MCQ Option Elimination via Profit-Markup Bounds
*   **When to Use**: MCQs with markup and discount parameters.
*   **How it Works**: 
    *   Net profit is **always** strictly less than the markup percentage if a discount is given.
    *   If a product is marked up by $30\%$ and sold with any discount, eliminate all options $\ge 30\%$ immediately.
