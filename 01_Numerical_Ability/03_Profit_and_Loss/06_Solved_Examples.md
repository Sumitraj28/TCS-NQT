# Profit & Loss Solved Examples

This guide contains 10 worked examples, ordered by difficulty from Easy to Hard.

---

## 1. Easy Solved Examples

**Q1.** An article is bought for ₹400 and sold for ₹480. Find the profit percentage.

*   **Recognition**: Direct comparison of Cost Price (CP) and Selling Price (SP) where SP > CP.
*   **Approach**: Use the basic profit percentage formula: $\text{Profit}\% = \frac{SP - CP}{CP} \times 100$.
*   **Step-by-step**:
    1. Identify variables: $CP = 400, SP = 480$.
    2. Find absolute profit: $480 - 400 = 80$.
    3. Calculate percentage:
       $$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{80}{400} \times 100 = 20\%$$
*   **Shortcut**:
    $$\text{Ratio } \frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{480}{400} = \frac{6}{5} \implies \frac{1}{5} \text{ gain} = 20\%$$
*   **Verification**: $20\%$ of $400$ is $80$. $400 + 80 = 480$. Correct.

---

**Q2.** The Marked Price of a watch is ₹1,500. If the shopkeeper offers a discount of $12\%$, find the Selling Price.

*   **Recognition**: Finding Selling Price when Marked Price (MP) and Discount% are known.
*   **Approach**: Use SP formula: $SP = MP \times (1 - \frac{D}{100})$.
*   **Step-by-step**:
    1. Identify variables: $MP = 1500, D = 12$.
    2. Write equation:
       $$SP = 1500 \times \left(1 - \frac{12}{100}\right)$$
    3. Calculate:
       $$SP = 1500 \times 0.88 = 1320$$
*   **Shortcut**:
    $$\text{Discount} = 10\% \text{ of } 1500 (150) + 2\% \text{ of } 1500 (30) = 180$$
    $$SP = 1500 - 180 = 1320$$
*   **Verification**: $\frac{1500 - 1320}{1500} \times 100 = \frac{180}{1500} \times 100 = 12\%$. Correct.

---

**Q3.** A store offers a "Buy 3, get 1 free" deal. What is the equivalent discount percentage?

*   **Recognition**: "Buy X, get Y free" promotional discount.
*   **Approach**: Apply the bundle discount formula: $\text{Discount}\% = \frac{\text{Free Items}}{\text{Total Items}} \times 100$.
*   **Step-by-step**:
    1. Identify variables: Free items $Y = 1$, paid items $X = 3$.
    2. Total items received: $X + Y = 3 + 1 = 4$.
    3. Calculate:
       $$\text{Discount}\% = \frac{1}{4} \times 100 = 25\%$$
*   **Shortcut**:
    $$\text{1 free out of 4 total} = 1/4 = 25\%$$
*   **Verification**: If each item costs ₹10, total value is ₹40. You pay for 3 items (₹30). Discount is ₹10. $\frac{10}{40} \times 100 = 25\%$. Correct.

## 2. Medium Solved Examples

**Q4.** A trader marks his goods up by $30\%$ above the Cost Price and then offers a discount of $10\%$. Find his net profit percentage.

*   **Recognition**: Successive markup-discount cycle on a single item.
*   **Approach**: Apply the successive percentage change formula: $m - d - \frac{md}{100}\%$.
*   **Step-by-step**:
    1. Identify variables: Markup $m = 30$, Discount $d = 10$.
    2. Write equation:
       $$\text{Net} = 30 - 10 - \frac{30 \times 10}{100}$$
    3. Calculate:
       $$\text{Net} = 20 - 3 = +17\%$$
*   **Shortcut**:
    $$CP = 100 \implies MP = 130 \implies \text{Discount} = 10\% \text{ of } 130 = 13$$
    $$SP = 130 - 13 = 117 \implies 17\% \text{ profit}$$
*   **Verification**: SP (117) is $17\%$ more than CP (100). Correct.

---

**Q5.** If the Cost Price of 18 articles is equal to the Selling Price of 15 articles, find the profit or loss percentage.

*   **Recognition**: "CP of X equals SP of Y" article ratio equation.
*   **Approach**: Equate cost and sale totals to form the CP/SP ratio: $X \times CP = Y \times SP$.
*   **Step-by-step**:
    1. Write equation: $18 \times CP = 15 \times SP$.
    2. Form ratio:
       $$\frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{18}{15} = \frac{6}{5}$$
    3. Since $SP > CP$, it is a profit:
       $$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{6-5}{5} \times 100 = \frac{1}{5} \times 100 = 20\%$$
*   **Shortcut**:
    $$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{18 - 15}{15} \times 100 = \frac{3}{15} \times 100 = 20\%$$
*   **Verification**: 18 articles at CP ₹10 each $= ₹180$. 15 articles sold at SP ₹12 each $= ₹180$. Profit is $\frac{12-10}{10} \times 100 = 20\%$. Correct.

---

**Q6.** A sells a laptop to B at a profit of $10\%$. B sells it to C at a loss of $5\%$. If C pays ₹20,900 for the laptop, how much did A pay for it?

*   **Recognition**: Consecutive transaction chain ($A \rightarrow B \rightarrow C$).
*   **Approach**: Use the Multiplying Factor (MF) chain equation.
*   **Step-by-step**:
    1. Set up multipliers: $MF_A = 1.10 = \frac{11}{10}$, $MF_B = 0.95 = \frac{19}{20}$.
    2. Set up equation:
       $$CP_A \times \frac{11}{10} \times \frac{19}{20} = 20,900$$
    3. Calculate:
       $$CP_A \times \frac{209}{200} = 20,900$$
       $$CP_A = 20,900 \times \frac{200}{209} = 100 \times 200 = ₹20,000$$
*   **Shortcut**:
    $$CP_A = \frac{20900}{1.1 \times 0.95} = \frac{20900}{1.045} = 20,000$$
*   **Verification**: A buys at 20,000 $\implies$ sells to B at 22,000 $\implies$ B sells to C at $22000 - 1100 = 20,900$. Correct.

---

**Q7.** Two calculators are sold for ₹9,900 each. The seller makes a $10\%$ profit on the first and a $10\%$ loss on the second. Find the net profit or loss percentage.

*   **Recognition**: Two items sold at the same Selling Price, with symmetric profit and loss rates ($+x\%$ and $-x\%$).
*   **Approach**: Apply the symmetric SP loss shortcut: $\text{Loss}\% = (\frac{x}{10})^2\%$.
*   **Step-by-step**:
    1. Identify profit/loss rate: $x = 10$.
    2. Apply formula:
       $$\text{Net Loss}\% = \left(\frac{10}{10}\right)^2\% = 1\%$$
*   **Shortcut**:
    $$\text{Loss} = 1^2 = 1\%$$
*   **Verification**: 
    *   $CP_1 = \frac{9900}{1.10} = 9000$.
    *   $CP_2 = \frac{9900}{0.90} = 11000$.
    *   Total CP $= 9000 + 11000 = 20000$.
    *   Total SP $= 9900 \times 2 = 19800$.
    *   $\text{Loss} = \frac{200}{20000} \times 100 = 1\%$. Correct.

## 3. Hard Solved Examples

**Q8.** A dishonest dealer marks his goods up by $20\%$ above Cost Price, but offers a discount of $10\%$. Additionally, he cheats his customers by using a weight of 900 grams instead of 1 kilogram. Find his net profit percentage.

*   **Recognition**: Multi-stage cheat combining markup, discount, and faulty weights.
*   **Approach**: Multiply the profit multiplying factors of the pricing stage and the weight stage.
*   **Step-by-step**:
    1. Calculate pricing factor: Markup $20\%$ and discount $10\%$ yields a net profit of $20 - 10 - \frac{200}{100} = 8\%$.
       $$MF_{\text{price}} = 1.08 = \frac{108}{100}$$
    2. Calculate weight factor: Dealer gives 900g instead of 1000g.
       $$MF_{\text{weight}} = \frac{\text{Weight Charged}}{\text{Weight Given}} = \frac{1000}{900} = \frac{10}{9}$$
    3. Multiply the factors:
       $$MF_{\text{net}} = \frac{108}{100} \times \frac{10}{9} = \frac{12}{10} = 1.20$$
    4. Convert multiplier back to percentage: $1.20 \implies 20\%$ profit.
*   **Shortcut**:
    $$\text{Pricing SP} = 108\text{ for 900g} \implies \text{Rate for 1000g} = 108 \times \frac{10}{9} = 120 \implies 20\%$$
*   **Verification**: Buying 900g costs ₹90 (at ₹100/kg). Sold for ₹108. Profit is $\frac{18}{90} \times 100 = 20\%$. Correct.

---

**Q9.** A merchant cheats by $10\%$ when buying goods from a wholesaler (using a heavier weight) and cheats by $10\%$ when selling to customers (using a lighter weight). If he sells at cost price, find his net profit percentage.

*   **Recognition**: Double-sided weight cheating (buying and selling stages).
*   **Approach**: Form the ratio of the actual goods received to the goods delivered: $\text{Profit}\% = \frac{\text{Goods Received} - \text{Goods Delivered}}{\text{Goods Delivered}} \times 100$.
*   **Step-by-step**:
    1. When buying, he cheats by $10\% \implies$ receives 1100 units while paying for 1000 units.
    2. When selling, he cheats by $10\% \implies$ delivers 900 units while charging for 1000 units.
    3. Establish the net transaction: He paid for 1000 units to get 1100 units, and then sells those 1100 units in batches of 900 units.
       $$\text{Profit}\% = \left(\frac{1100 - 900}{900}\right) \times 100 = \frac{200}{900} \times 100 = 22.22\%$$
*   **Shortcut**:
    $$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{\% \text{Cheat Buy} + \% \text{Cheat Sell}}{100 - \% \text{Cheat Sell}} \times 100 = \frac{10 + 10}{100 - 10} \times 100 = \frac{20}{90} \times 100 = 22.22\%$$
*   **Verification**: Ratio of goods received to delivered $= \frac{1.10}{0.90} = 1.2222 \implies 22.22\%$ profit. Correct.

**Q10.** A shopkeeper sells an article at a profit of $20\%$. If he had bought it at $10\%$ less and sold it for ₹30 less, he would have gained $25\%$. Find the Cost Price of the article.

*   **Recognition**: Hypothetical transaction shift with absolute price difference.
*   **Approach**: Write the SP equations for both the actual and hypothetical scenarios and solve for CP.
*   **Step-by-step**:
    1. Set up actual scenario: Let $CP_1 = 100x$. Profit is $20\% \implies SP_1 = 120x$.
    2. Set up hypothetical scenario:
       *   New CP ($CP_2$): $10\%$ less $\implies CP_2 = 90x$.
       *   New SP ($SP_2$): Sold for ₹30 less $\implies SP_2 = 120x - 30$.
    3. Apply profit rate constraint on hypothetical scenario: Profit is $25\%$.
       $$SP_2 = CP_2 \times 1.25$$
       $$120x - 30 = 90x \times 1.25$$
       $$120x - 30 = 112.5x$$
    4. Solve for $x$:
       $$120x - 112.5x = 30 \implies 7.5x = 30 \implies x = 4$$
    5. Find original CP:
       $$CP_1 = 100x = 100(4) = ₹400$$
*   **Shortcut**:
    $$120\%CP - 30 = 90\%CP \times 1.25 \implies 120\%CP - 30 = 112.5\%CP$$
    $$7.5\%CP = 30 \implies CP = \frac{30}{7.5} \times 100 = 400$$
*   **Verification**: CP $= 400 \implies SP = 480$. If bought for $360$ ($10\%$ less) and sold for $450$ (30 less), profit is $\frac{90}{360} \times 100 = 25\%$. Correct.
