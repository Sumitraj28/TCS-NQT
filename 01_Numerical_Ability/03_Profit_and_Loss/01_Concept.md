# Profit & Loss Concept Guide

## 1. The Core Idea: Cost & Value
Profit and Loss is how we track whether buying and selling something makes us richer or poorer compared to what we spent to get it. If you sell an item for more than you spent to buy it, you make a profit; if you sell it for less, you suffer a loss.

### Real-World Anchor: The Used Mobile Phone
Suppose you buy a smartphone for ₹10,000. 
*   If you use it and later sell it to a friend for ₹8,000, you have lost ₹2,000 in value. 
*   If you buy a rare vintage watch for ₹10,000 and find a collector willing to buy it for ₹15,000, you have gained ₹5,000 in profit.

---

## 2. Formal Definitions
To solve exam questions, you must learn the standard vocabulary of commercial trades:

*   **Cost Price (CP)**: The total money spent by the buyer to acquire the product. This includes the manufacturing cost or purchase price plus any overhead expenses like transportation, repairs, or setup costs.
*   **Selling Price (SP)**: The final amount of money a seller receives when transferring ownership of the product to a customer.
*   **Marked Price (MP)**: The advertised retail price (also called list price or maximum retail price, MRP). This is the baseline from which discounts are subtracted.
*   **Profit (Gain)**: The positive financial gain when SP is greater than CP.
*   **Loss**: The financial deficit when CP is greater than SP.
*   **Discount**: The deduction offered on the Marked Price to entice customers.
*   **Markup**: The amount by which the cost price is increased to set the Marked Price.

---

## 3. Core Insights
CP and MP are the two anchors for all percentage calculations in this topic. 

```
  [Cost Price (CP)] ───────── Markup % ─────────> [Marked Price (MP)]
         │                                              │
         │                                          Discount %
      Profit %                                          │
         │                                              v
         └───────────────> [Selling Price (SP)] <───────┘
```

> [!IMPORTANT]
> *   **Profit/Loss Percentage is ALWAYS calculated on Cost Price (CP)** unless the question explicitly specifies otherwise.
> *   **Discount Percentage is ALWAYS calculated on Marked Price (MP)**.

---

## 4. Concept Breakdown Table

| Transaction Action | Mathematical Expression | Percentage Calculation | Base reference (100%) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Markup** | $$MP = CP + \text{Markup}$$ | $$\text{Markup}\% = \frac{\text{Markup}}{CP} \times 100$$ | Cost Price (CP) |
| **Discount** | $$SP = MP - \text{Discount}$$ | $$\text{Discount}\% = \frac{\text{Discount}}{MP} \times 100$$ | Marked Price (MP) |
| **Profit** | $$SP = CP + \text{Profit}$$ | $$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{\text{Profit}}{CP} \times 100$$ | Cost Price (CP) |
| **Loss** | $$SP = CP - \text{Loss}$$ | $$\text{Loss}\% = \frac{\text{Loss}}{CP} \times 100$$ | Cost Price (CP) |

---

## 5. Connections to Other Topics
Profit and Loss is a direct extension of **Percentages**. A profit of $20\%$ means SP is $120\%$ of CP (multiplying factor $= 1.20$). A discount of $15\%$ means SP is $85\%$ of MP (multiplying factor $= 0.85$). Understanding base-adjustments (percentages) makes Profit & Loss calculations trivial.

---

## 6. Historical Note: Double-Entry Bookkeeping
The mathematical structuring of profits and losses was popularized by the Italian mathematician **Luca Pacioli** in 1494. Known as the "Father of Accounting," Pacioli collaborated with Leonardo da Vinci and published the first systemized rules of debits, credits, and ledger sheets, which are still used by modern corporate accounting systems today.
