# Profit & Loss Intermediate Practice

This set contains 20 intermediate-level questions requiring 1–2 steps of reasoning.

---

**Q1.** Find the equivalent single discount for successive discounts of $20\%$ and $10\%$.
> 🎯 Hint: Apply $d_{\text{net}} = d_1 + d_2 - \frac{d_1 d_2}{100}$.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
$$\text{Net Discount} = 20 + 10 - \frac{20 \times 10}{100} = 30 - 2 = 28\%$$
**Answer:** **28**  
**Shortcut:** $20 + 10 - 2 = 28\%$.  
**Common Mistake:** Adding them linearly: $20\% + 10\% = 30\%$.
</details>

**Q2.** If the Cost Price of 16 articles equals the Selling Price of 12 articles, find the profit percentage.
> 🎯 Hint: Write $16 \times CP = 12 \times SP$ and isolate the ratio.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
$$\frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{16}{12} = \frac{4}{3} \implies \text{Profit} = \frac{4-3}{3} \times 100 = 33.33\%$$
**Answer:** **33.33**  
**Shortcut:** $\frac{16 - 12}{12} \times 100 = 33.33\%$.  
**Common Mistake:** Dividing by 16: $\frac{4}{16} \times 100 = 25\%$.
</details>

**Q3.** A trader marks up his goods by $30\%$ and offers a $15\%$ discount. Find his net profit percentage.
> 🎯 Hint: Apply $m - d - \frac{md}{100}\%$.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
$$\text{Net} = 30 - 15 - \frac{30 \times 15}{100} = 15 - 4.5 = 10.5\%$$
**Answer:** **10.5**  
**Shortcut:** $15 - 4.5 = 10.5\%$.  
**Common Mistake:** Subtracting percentages linearly: $30\% - 15\% = 15\%$.
</details>

**Q4.** A shopkeeper sells sugar at cost price but uses a weight of 920g instead of 1kg. Find his profit percentage.
> 🎯 Hint: Profit is calculated on delivered weight (920g).
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
$$\text{Error} = 80\text{g}, \quad \text{Delivered} = 920\text{g}$$
$$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{80}{920} \times 100 = \frac{2}{23} \times 100 \approx 8.7\%$$
**Answer:** **8.7**  
**Shortcut:** $\frac{80}{920} \approx 8.7\%$.  
**Common Mistake:** Dividing by 1000g: $\frac{80}{1000} \times 100 = 8\%$.
</details>

**Q5.** Two items are sold at ₹5,000 each. One makes $15\%$ profit and the other $15\%$ loss. Find net loss percentage.
> 🎯 Hint: Use the symmetric SP shortcut: $(\frac{x}{10})^2\%$.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
$$\text{Net Loss}\% = \left(\frac{15}{10}\right)^2\% = 1.5^2\% = 2.25\%$$
**Answer:** **2.25**  
**Shortcut:** $1.5^2 = 2.25\%$.  
**Common Mistake:** Stating "No profit, no loss" (break-even).
</details>

**Q6.** A sells a book to B at a $20\%$ profit. B sells it to C at a $10\%$ loss. If C pays ₹972, find A's Cost Price.
> 🎯 Hint: Set up $CP_A \times 1.20 \times 0.90 = 972$.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
$$CP_A \times 1.08 = 972 \implies CP_A = \frac{972}{1.08} = 900$$
**Answer:** **900**  
**Shortcut:** $972 / 1.08 = 900$.  
**Common Mistake:** Multiplying 972 by $0.80 \times 1.10$.
</details>

**Q7.** What is the discount percentage for a "Buy 5, get 2 free" promotion?
> 🎯 Hint: Divide 2 free items by 7 total items.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
$$\text{Discount}\% = \frac{2}{5+2} \times 100 = \frac{2}{7} \times 100 = 28.57\%$$
**Answer:** **28.57**  
**Shortcut:** $2/7 \approx 28.57\%$.  
**Common Mistake:** Using $2/5 = 40\%$ discount.
</details>

**Q8.** An article is sold at a loss of $10\%$. If sold for ₹90 more, the seller makes a profit of $5\%$. Find the Cost Price.
> 🎯 Hint: The absolute price difference of ₹90 corresponds to a $15\%$ percentage span change.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
$$\text{Span} = +5\% - (-10\%) = 15\%$$
$$15\% \text{ of CP} = 90 \implies CP = \frac{90}{15} \times 100 = 600$$
**Answer:** **600**  
**Shortcut:** $90 / 0.15 = 600$.  
**Common Mistake:** Subtracting rates: $10\% - 5\% = 5\% \implies CP = 1800$.
</details>

**Q9.** A merchant marks up his goods by $50\%$ and offers a $20\%$ discount. Find his net profit percentage.
> 🎯 Hint: Apply $50 - 20 - \frac{1000}{100}\%$.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
$$\text{Net} = 50 - 20 - 10 = 20\%$$
**Answer:** **20**  
**Shortcut:** $50 - 20 - 10 = 20\%$.  
**Common Mistake:** Assuming $50\% - 20\% = 30\%$.
</details>

**Q10.** By selling a speaker for ₹720, a dealer loses $10\%$. What should be the Selling Price to make a profit of $20\%$?
> 🎯 Hint: SP is proportional to $(100 \pm P/L)\%$.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
$$90\% \text{ of CP} = 720 \implies CP = 800$$
$$\text{New SP} = 800 \times 1.20 = 960$$
**Answer:** **960**  
**Shortcut:** $720 \times \frac{120}{90} = 720 \times \frac{4}{3} = 960$.  
**Common Mistake:** Adding $30\%$ directly to ₹720 (₹936).
</details>

**Q11.** If a book is sold at a profit of $15\%$ instead of a $10\%$ loss, the seller receives ₹75 more. Find the Cost Price.
> 🎯 Hint: The span between $-10\%$ and $+15\%$ is $25\%$.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
$$25\% \text{ of CP} = 75 \implies CP = 75 \times 4 = 300$$
**Answer:** **300**  
**Shortcut:** $75 / 0.25 = 300$.  
**Common Mistake:** Using $15\% - 10\% = 5\% \implies CP = 1500$.
</details>

**Q13.** Marked Price of an item is ₹800. After two successive discounts of $10\%$ and $x\%$, it is sold for ₹612. Find $x$.
> 🎯 Hint: Find the intermediate price after the first $10\%$ discount.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Price after $10\%$ discount: $800 - 80 = 720$.  
2. Second discount: $720 - 612 = 108$.  
3. $x\% = \frac{108}{720} \times 100 = 15\%$.  
**Answer:** **15**  
**Shortcut:** $\frac{108}{720} = \frac{3}{20} = 15\%$.  
**Common Mistake:** Calculating the second discount relative to ₹800: $\frac{108}{800} \times 100 = 13.5\%$.
</details>

**Q14.** A man sells two articles for ₹1,850 each. He gains $25\%$ on one and loses $25\%$ on the other. Find the net loss percentage.

<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
$$\text{Loss}\% = \frac{25^2}{100} = 6.25\%$$
**Answer:** **6.25**

</details>

**Q15.** The Cost Price of 8 articles equals the Selling Price of 10 articles. Find the profit or loss percentage.
> 🎯 Hint: Write $8 \times CP = 10 \times SP$ and form the ratio.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
$$\frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{8}{10} = 0.8 \implies 20\%\text{ Loss}$$
**Answer:** **20**  
**Shortcut:** $\frac{10-8}{10} \times 100 = 20\%$ loss.  
**Common Mistake:** Writing $\frac{2}{8} \times 100 = 25\%$ profit.
</details>

**Q16.** A shopkeeper marks up his goods by $25\%$ and offers a discount. He still gains $10\%$. Find the discount percentage.
> 🎯 Hint: Write $1.25 \times (1 - D) = 1.10$.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
$$1 - D = \frac{1.10}{1.25} = \frac{110}{125} = 0.88 \implies D = 0.12 = 12\%$$
**Answer:** **12**  
**Shortcut:** $\frac{\text{Markup} - \text{Profit}}{100 + \text{Markup}} \times 100 = \frac{15}{125} \times 100 = 12\%$.  
**Common Mistake:** Subtracting rates: $25\% - 10\% = 15\%$.
</details>

**Q19.** By selling 33 meters of cloth, a merchant gains the Selling Price of 11 meters. Find the profit percentage.
> 🎯 Hint: Write $SP(33) - CP(33) = SP(11)$.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
$$33 \times SP - 33 \times CP = 11 \times SP \implies 22 \times SP = 33 \times CP$$
$$\frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{33}{22} = \frac{3}{20} \text{ ??? correction: } \frac{3}{2} \implies 50\%\text{ Profit}$$
**Answer:** **50**  
**Shortcut:** $\frac{11}{33 - 11} \times 100 = \frac{11}{22} \times 100 = 50\%$.  
**Common Mistake:** Using $\frac{11}{33} \times 100 = 33.33\%$.
</details>

**Q20.** By selling 45 lemons for ₹40, a man loses $20\%$. How many lemons should he sell for ₹24 to gain $20\%$?
> 🎯 Hint: Set up rate per lemon equation: $\text{Rate}_1 \times \frac{1.20}{0.80} = \text{Rate}_2$.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Rate per lemon at $20\%$ loss: $\frac{40}{45} = \frac{8}{9}$ per lemon.  
2. Target rate per lemon: $\frac{8}{9} \times \frac{1.20}{0.80} = \frac{8}{9} \times \frac{3}{2} = \frac{4}{3}$ per lemon.  
3. Number of lemons for ₹24: $\frac{24}{4/3} = 24 \times \frac{3}{4} = 18$ lemons.  
**Answer:** **18**  
**Shortcut:** $\frac{45 \times 40 \times 1.2}{0.8 \times 24} \implies 18$.  
**Common Mistake:** Reversing the loss/profit ratio multiplier.
</details>
