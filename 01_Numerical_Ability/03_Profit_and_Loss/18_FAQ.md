# Profit & Loss FAQ

Here are the answers to the 6 most frequently asked questions about Profit & Loss for the TCS NQT.

---

### Q1. Why is Profit/Loss percentage calculated on Cost Price (CP)?
**Answer**: 
Profit and Loss percentages measure the financial efficiency or rate of return of an investment. Since the Cost Price (CP) represents the total capital invested to acquire or create the product, the return must be measured relative to the size of this starting investment. 

---

### Q2. How do successive discounts work?
**Answer**: 
Successive discounts are applied in stages. The first discount is subtracted from the original Marked Price. The second discount is then calculated and subtracted from this new, reduced intermediate price, not the original price. Because the second base is smaller, successive discounts of $20\%$ and $10\%$ yield a net discount of $28\%$, which is less than the linear sum of $30\%$.

---

### Q3. Why does the dishonest dealer formula use delivered weight in the denominator?
**Answer**: 
The dealer's actual investment is the weight of the goods he physically gives to the customer. For example, if a dealer cheats by using 900g instead of 1kg, he only spends capital to buy/deliver 900g of goods. Therefore, his profit must be calculated using 900g as the Cost Price baseline.

---

### Q4. How do I solve "Buy 3, get 1 free" offers?
**Answer**: 
Treat the entire bundle as the Marked Price and the free item as the discount value.
*   Total items received $= 3 + 1 = 4$.
*   Free items received $= 1$.
*   Discount percentage $= \frac{1}{4} \times 100 = 25\%$.

---

### Q5. What is the CP-SP ratio trick?
**Answer**: 
When a question states "CP of X articles = SP of Y articles," write the equation $X \times CP = Y \times SP$. Transpose the terms to form the ratio:
$$\frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{X}{Y}$$
If the fraction $\frac{X}{Y} > 1$, it represents a profit; if $< 1$, it is a loss.

---

### Q6. How should I format my numerical answers on the TCS NQT exam terminal?
**Answer**: 
Type raw numbers only. Do not include trailing spaces, units, or currency symbols.
*   **Correct**: `25`
*   **Incorrect**: `25%`, `₹25`, `25.0`, `25 percent`
Typing any symbols or characters will cause the automated grading compiler to fail your answer.
