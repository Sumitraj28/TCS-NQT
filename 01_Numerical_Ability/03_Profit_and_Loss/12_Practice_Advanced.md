# Profit & Loss Advanced Practice

This set contains 20 advanced-level questions involving multi-concept reasoning.

---

**Q1.** A dishonest dealer marks up his goods by $30\%$ and offers a $10\%$ discount. Additionally, he cheats on weight by delivering 850g instead of 1kg. Find his net profit percentage.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "marks up... discount... weight of 850g instead of 1kg". Multi-cheat problem.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Pricing factor: $m=30, d=10 \implies \text{Net Price Profit} = 30 - 10 - \frac{300}{100} = 17\%$.  
   $$MF_{\text{price}} = 1.17$$  
2. Weight factor: Gives 850g instead of 1000g.  
   $$MF_{\text{weight}} = \frac{1000}{850} = \frac{20}{17}$$  
3. Combine factors:  
   $$MF_{\text{net}} = 1.17 \times \frac{20}{17} = \frac{117}{100} \times \frac{20}{17} = \frac{9 \times 20}{100} = \frac{180}{100} = 1.80$$  
   Net profit is $80\%$.  
**Answer:** **80**  
**Alternative Approach:** SP for 850g $= 117 \implies$ SP for 1000g $= 117 \times \frac{1000}{850} = 180 \implies 80\%$ profit.  
**What makes it hard:** Forgetting that the weight cheating acts as a compounding factor on top of the markup-discount price.
</details>

**Q2.** A man sells an article at a profit of $10\%$. If he had bought it at $20\%$ less and sold it for ₹20 more, he would have made a profit of $40\%$. Find the Cost Price of the article.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "bought it at 20% less and sold it for ₹20 more... profit of 40%". Hypothetical shift.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Let $CP_1 = 100x \implies SP_1 = 110x$.  
2. $CP_2 = 80x$, $SP_2 = 110x + 20$.  
3. Profit $= 40\% \implies SP_2 = CP_2 \times 1.40$.  
   $$110x + 20 = 80x \times 1.40 = 112x$$  
   $$2x = 20 \implies x = 10$$  
4. $CP_1 = 100x = 100(10) = ₹1,000$.  
**Answer:** **1000**  
**Alternative Approach:** $1.10CP + 20 = 0.80CP \times 1.40 \implies 1.12CP - 1.10CP = 20 \implies 0.02CP = 20 \implies CP = 1000$.  
**What makes it hard:** Tracking the shift of the CP base from $100\%$ to $80\%$ when calculating the hypothetical profit.
</details>

**Q3.** A shopkeeper marks up his goods such that even after giving a discount of $25\%$, he makes a profit of $20\%$. Find the markup percentage.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "discount of 25%... profit of 20%... find markup".
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Use ratio: $\frac{MP}{CP} = \frac{100 + P\%}{100 - D\%}$.  
2. Calculate:  
   $$\frac{MP}{CP} = \frac{120}{75} = \frac{8}{5} = 1.60$$  
   Markup is $(1.60 - 1) \times 100 = 60\%$.  
**Answer:** **60**  
**Alternative Approach:** Let CP = 100. Profit = 20% $\implies$ SP = 120. Discount = 25% $\implies 0.75MP = 120 \implies MP = 160 \implies 60\%$ markup.  
**What makes it hard:** Understanding that the discount is calculated on MP while profit is on CP.
</details>

**Q4.** A merchant sells an article at $20\%$ profit. If both the Cost Price and Selling Price are reduced by ₹100, the profit would be $4\%$ more. Find the original Cost Price.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "CP and SP are both reduced by ₹100... profit 4% more". Double reduction shift.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Let $CP_1 = x \implies SP_1 = 1.20x$.  
2. $CP_2 = x - 100$, $SP_2 = 1.20x - 100$.  
3. New profit $= 20\% + 4\% = 24\% \implies SP_2 = CP_2 \times 1.24$.  
   $$1.20x - 100 = 1.24(x - 100)$$  
   $$1.20x - 100 = 1.24x - 124$$  
   $$0.04x = 24 \implies x = \frac{24}{0.04} = 600$$  
**Answer:** **600**  
**Alternative Approach:** Let ratio $SP_1 : CP_1 = 6:5$. $SP_2 : CP_2 = 124:100 = 31:25$.  
$$\frac{6y - 100}{5y - 100} = \frac{31}{25} \implies 150y - 2500 = 153y - 3100 \implies 3y = 600 \implies y = 200 \implies CP = 1000?? \text{ No, } CP = 5y = 5(120) = 600 \text{ (correct)}.$$  
**What makes it hard:** Setting up cross-multiplication with algebraic shifts.
</details>

**Q5.** A dealer cheats by $15\%$ when buying goods and cheats by $15\%$ when selling goods using faulty weights. If he sells at cost price, find his net profit percentage.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "cheats by 15% when buying... cheats by 15% when selling". Double-sided cheat.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Buying cheat: gets 115 units while paying for 100.  
2. Selling cheat: delivers 85 units while charging for 100.  
3. Net profit ratio:  
   $$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{115 - 85}{85} \times 100 = \frac{30}{85} \times 100 = \frac{6}{17} \times 100 = 35.29\%$$  
**Answer:** **35.29**  
**Alternative Approach:** $\frac{15 + 15}{100 - 15} \times 100 = \frac{30}{85} \times 100 = 35.29\%$.  
**What makes it hard:** Confusing the buying cheat as a reduction in CP instead of an increase in goods volume.
</details>

**Q6.** A dealer sells $\frac{2}{3}\text{rd}$ of his goods at $30\%$ profit, $\frac{1}{6}\text{th}$ at $12\%$ profit, and the rest at an $18\%$ loss. Find his net profit percentage.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: Fractional goods division with different profit/loss rates.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Remaining goods fraction: $1 - (\frac{2}{3} + \frac{1}{6}) = 1 - \frac{5}{6} = \frac{1}{6}$.  
2. Apply weighted average profit:  
   $$\text{Net Profit}\% = \left(\frac{2}{3} \times 30\right) + \left(\frac{1}{6} \times 12\right) + \left(\frac{1}{6} \times (-18)\right)$$  
   $$\text{Net} = 20 + 2 - 3 = 19\%$$  
**Answer:** **19**  
**Alternative Approach:** Assume total goods $= 6$ units worth ₹100 each. Total CP $= 600$.  
*   4 units sold at $30\%$ profit $= 520$.  
*   1 unit sold at $12\%$ profit $= 112$.  
*   1 unit sold at $18\%$ loss $= 82$.  
*   Total SP $= 520 + 112 + 82 = 714 \implies 19\%$ profit.  
**What makes it hard:** Finding the remaining fractional partition and keeping track of the negative sign for losses.
</details>

**Q7.** Marked Price of an item is ₹800. Two successive discounts are offered, the first is $10\%$ and the second is $x\%$. If the Selling Price is ₹576, find $x$.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "two successive discounts... first is 10%, second is x%". Finding missing rate.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. SP after 1st discount: $800 \times 0.90 = 720$.  
2. Value of 2nd discount: $720 - 576 = 144$.  
3. Calculate rate:  
   $$x\% = \frac{144}{720} \times 100 = 20\%$$  
**Answer:** **20**  
**Alternative Approach:** $800 \times 0.90 \times (1 - x/100) = 576 \implies 7.20 \times (100 - x) = 576 \implies 100 - x = 80 \implies x = 20$.  
**What makes it hard:** Calculating the second discount percentage on the starting ₹800 base instead of the intermediate ₹720.
</details>

**Q8.** A merchant bought two cycles for ₹2,000. He sold one at a $20\%$ loss and the other at a $20\%$ profit. If the Selling Prices of both are equal, find their individual cost prices.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "bought two cycles for ₹2000... equal Selling Prices". Equal SP, asymmetric CP.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Let cost prices be $CP_1$ and $CP_2$.  
   $$CP_1 + CP_2 = 2000$$  
2. Equate Selling Prices:  
   $$CP_1 \times 0.80 = CP_2 \times 1.20 \implies \frac{CP_1}{CP_2} = \frac{1.20}{0.80} = \frac{3}{2}$$  
3. Divide ₹2,000 in the ratio $3:2$:  
   $$CP_1 = 2000 \times \frac{3}{5} = ₹1,200$$  
   $$CP_2 = 2000 \times \frac{2}{5} = ₹800$$  
**Answer:** **1200, 800**  
**Alternative Approach:** Ratio split directly: $CP_1 = \frac{100+20}{200} \times 2000$ (No, use inverse ratio).  
**What makes it hard:** Reversing the ratio division (giving the larger cost to the profit item instead of the loss item).
</details>

**Q9.** A man bought a certain number of articles at 3 for ₹10 and sold them at 4 for ₹15. Find his profit or loss percentage.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "bought at 3 for ₹10... sold at 4 for ₹15". Rate count matching.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Equate the count of articles. LCM of 3 and 4 is 12.  
2. Find CP of 12 articles:  
   $$CP = \left(\frac{10}{3}\right) \times 12 = ₹40$$  
3. Find SP of 12 articles:  
   $$SP = \left(\frac{15}{4}\right) \times 12 = ₹45$$  
4. Calculate profit:  
   $$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{45 - 40}{40} \times 100 = \frac{5}{40} \times 100 = 12.5\%$$  
**Answer:** **12.5**  
**Alternative Approach:** Cross multiply: $\frac{10}{3}$ and $\frac{15}{4} \implies 10 \times 4 = 40$ (CP) and $15 \times 3 = 45$ (SP) $\implies \frac{5}{40} = 12.5\%$.  
**What makes it hard:** Comparing fractional prices directly without aligning unit quantity counts.
</details>

**Q10.** A shopkeeper sells an item at a $10\%$ profit. If he increases the price by ₹20, he gains $15\%$. If he wants to gain $25\%$, what should be the Selling Price?
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "price increase of ₹20... gains 15% instead of 10%". Span calculation.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. The $5\%$ gain increase corresponds to the ₹20 price increase.  
   $$5\% \text{ of CP} = 20 \implies CP = 400$$  
2. Target profit $= 25\%$:  
   $$\text{Target SP} = 400 \times 1.25 = ₹500$$  
**Answer:** **500**  
**Alternative Approach:** $SP_2 = 720 \times \frac{125}{105}$ (No, using difference directly is fastest).  
**What makes it hard:** Finding the CP first instead of calculating the target SP directly.
</details>

**Q11.** The Selling Price of 5 articles is equal to the Cost Price of 6 articles. If the shopkeeper offers a discount of $5\%$ on the Marked Price, he gains $14\%$. Find the markup percentage.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "SP of 5 = CP of 6... discount of 5%... gains 14%". Combined ratio.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. From first clause, find nominal SP (un-discounted):  
   $$5 \times SP_0 = 6 \times CP \implies \frac{SP_0}{CP} = \frac{6}{5} = 1.20$$  
   This means the nominal profit without discount would be $20\%$.  
2. We know: $SP = MP \times 0.95 = CP \times 1.14$.  
   $$\frac{MP}{CP} = \frac{1.14}{0.95} = 1.20 \implies 20\% \text{ Markup}$$  
**Answer:** **20**  
**Alternative Approach:** $\frac{MP}{CP} = \frac{100+14}{100-5} = \frac{114}{95} = 1.20 \implies 20\%$ markup.  
**What makes it hard:** Sifting through the redundant clause "SP of 5 = CP of 6" which is included by TCS as a distractor.
</details>

**Q12.** A merchant sells rice at a $10\%$ profit, but uses a weight that is $10\%$ less than nominal. Additionally, he mixes it with sand (costs ₹0) in a ratio of $9:1$. Find his net profit percentage.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "sells at 10% profit... weight 10% less... mixes with sand in ratio 9:1". Multi-layered fraud.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Pricing factor: sells at $10\%$ profit $\implies MF_1 = 1.10$.  
2. Weight factor: uses $10\%$ less weight $\implies$ delivers 900g for 1000g $\implies MF_2 = \frac{10}{9}$.  
3. Adulteration factor: mixes sand in ratio $9:1 \implies$ sells 10 units of volume but only 9 units are actual rice:  
   $$MF_3 = \frac{10}{9}$$  
4. Multiply all factors:  
   $$MF_{\text{net}} = \frac{11}{10} \times \frac{10}{9} \times \frac{10}{9} = \frac{110}{81} \approx 1.358 \implies 35.8\% \text{ Profit}$$  
**Answer:** **35.8**  
**Alternative Approach:** Track 900g of pure rice. Adulterated with 100g sand $= 1000$g mixture. Sold as 1111g (due to $10\%$ weight cheat) at $10\%$ profit $\implies 1111 \times 1.1 \approx 1222 \implies$ profit is $\frac{1222 - 900}{900} = 35.8\%$.  
**What makes it hard:** Multiplying the sand adulteration factor as a volume multiplier.
</details>

**Q13.** A dealer sells an article at a $15\%$ profit. If he had bought it at $10\%$ less and sold it for ₹4 less, he would have gained $25\%$. Find the Cost Price.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "bought at 10% less... sold for ₹4 less... gained 25%". Hypothetical shift.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Actual: $CP_1 = 100x \implies SP_1 = 115x$.  
2. Hypothetical: $CP_2 = 90x$, $SP_2 = 115x - 4$.  
3. Equate:  
   $$115x - 4 = 90x \times 1.25 = 112.5x$$  
   $$2.5x = 4 \implies x = 1.6$$  
4. $CP_1 = 100x = 100(1.6) = ₹160$.  
**Answer:** **160**  
**Alternative Approach:** $1.15CP - 4 = 0.90CP \times 1.25 = 1.125CP \implies 0.025CP = 4 \implies CP = 160$.  
**What makes it hard:** Calculating fractions with decimals.
</details>

**Q14.** Two items are sold at ₹9,900 each, one at $10\%$ profit and the other at $10\%$ loss. What is the total loss or profit value in rupees?
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "sold at ₹9900 each... total loss or profit in rupees". Same SP.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Symmetric SP net loss percentage $= (\frac{10}{10})^2\% = 1\%$ Loss.  
2. Since loss is $1\%$ of Total CP, then Total SP $= 99\%$ of Total CP.  
   $$19800 = 0.99 \times \text{Total CP} \implies \text{Total CP} = 20,000$$  
3. Absolute Loss $= \text{Total CP} - \text{Total SP} = 20,000 - 19,800 = ₹200$.  
**Answer:** **200**  
**Alternative Approach:** Calculate CP values: $9900/1.1 = 9000$ and $9900/0.9 = 11000$. Total CP $= 20,000$. Total SP $= 19,800 \implies$ Loss $= 200$.  
**What makes it hard:** Calculating the loss percentage on the SP base instead of CP base.
</details>

**Q15.** A reduction of $20\%$ in the price of sugar enables a purchaser to obtain 4kg more for ₹160. Find the original price of sugar per kg.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "reduction of 20%... 4kg more for ₹160". Price-consumption shift.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Price reduction value $= 20\% \text{ of } 160 = ₹32$.  
2. This ₹32 savings buys the extra 4kg of sugar.  
   $$\text{Reduced Price} = \frac{32}{4} = ₹8/\text{kg}$$  
3. Since price was reduced by $20\%$ (multiplier $= 0.8$):  
   $$\text{Original Price} = \frac{8}{0.8} = ₹10/\text{kg}$$  
**Answer:** **10**  
**Alternative Approach:** Let original price be $P$. Consumption $= \frac{160}{P}$.  
$$\frac{160}{0.8P} - \frac{160}{P} = 4 \implies \frac{200}{P} - \frac{160}{P} = 4 \implies \frac{40}{P} = 4 \implies P = 10$$.  
**What makes it hard:** Confusing the reduced price (₹8/kg) with the original price (₹10/kg).
</details>

**Q16.** A shopkeeper offers a discount of $10\%$ on the Marked Price. If he sells for ₹900, he makes a profit of $25\%$ on the Cost Price. If no discount is offered, what is his profit percentage?
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "discount of 10%... sells for 900... profit of 25%... if no discount...".
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. If $SP = 900$ and discount $= 10\% \implies 0.90MP = 900 \implies MP = 1000$.  
2. If profit $= 25\% \implies 1.25CP = 900 \implies CP = \frac{900}{1.25} = 720$.  
3. If no discount is offered, $SP = MP = 1000$.  
   $$\text{New Profit}\% = \frac{1000 - 720}{720} \times 100 = \frac{280}{720} \times 100 = 38.89\%$$  
**Answer:** **38.89**  
**Alternative Approach:** $\frac{MP}{CP} = \frac{125}{90} = 1.3889 \implies 38.89\%$.  
**What makes it hard:** Keeping track of the shifting Selling Price bases.
</details>

**Q17.** A man sells an article at $5\%$ profit. If he had bought it at $5\%$ less and sold it for ₹1 less, he would have gained $10\%$. Find the Cost Price.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "bought at 5% less and sold for ₹1 less... gained 10%". Hypothetical shift.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Actual: $CP_1 = 100x \implies SP_1 = 105x$.  
2. Hypothetical: $CP_2 = 95x$, $SP_2 = 105x - 1$.  
3. Equate:  
   $$105x - 1 = 95x \times 1.10 = 104.5x$$  
   $$0.5x = 1 \implies x = 2$$  
4. $CP_1 = 100x = 100(2) = ₹200$.  
**Answer:** **200**  
**Alternative Approach:** $1.05CP - 1 = 0.95CP \times 1.10 = 1.045CP \implies 0.005CP = 1 \implies CP = 200$.  
**What makes it hard:** Handling small decimal percentages like $0.5\%$.
</details>

**Q18.** A merchant bought two items for a total of ₹1,200. He sold one at a $20\%$ loss and the other at a $30\%$ profit. If there is no net profit or loss on the whole transaction, find the cost prices of both.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "no net profit or loss... total cost ₹1200". Alligation allocation.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Since net profit is $0\%$, apply alligation:  
```
  Loss (-20)          Profit (+30)
              \       /
                Net (0)
              /       \
            30         20
```
2. Ratio of cost prices is $30:20 = 3:2$.  
3. Divide ₹1,200 in the ratio $3:2$:  
   $$CP_1 = 1200 \times \frac{3}{5} = ₹720$$  
   $$CP_2 = 1200 \times \frac{2}{5} = ₹480$$  
**Answer:** **720, 480**  
**Alternative Approach:** $0.20 \times CP_1 = 0.30 \times CP_2 \implies \frac{CP_1}{CP_2} = \frac{3}{2}$.  
**What makes it hard:** Assigning the correct cost price to the correct item.
</details>

**Q19.** By selling 33 meters of cloth, a merchant loses the Cost Price of 11 meters. Find the loss percentage.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "loses the Cost Price of 11 meters".
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Write equation: $CP(33) - SP(33) = CP(11)$.  
2. Simplify:  
   $$22 \times CP = 33 \times SP \implies \frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{22}{33} = \frac{2}{3}$$  
3. Loss percentage:  
   $$\text{Loss}\% = \frac{3-2}{3} \times 100 = 33.33\%$$  
**Answer:** **33.33**  
**Alternative Approach:** Loss is directly $\frac{11}{33} \times 100 = 33.33\%$.  
**What makes it hard:** Differentiating whether the gain/loss is in terms of Cost Price or Selling Price.
</details>

**Q20.** A dishonest dealer marks up his goods by $25\%$ and sells them at cost price, but uses a weight of 800 grams instead of 1 kilogram. Find his net profit percentage.
> 🎯 Recognition Clue: "marks up by 25%... sells at cost... weight of 800g instead of 1kg".
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>
**Step-by-step:**  
1. Wait! The clause says "marks up by 25% and sells them at cost price". If he sells at cost price, the markup is ignored because the final SP equals the original CP.  
2. He only makes profit from weight cheating: uses 800g instead of 1kg.  
   $$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{200}{800} \times 100 = 25\%$$  
**Answer:** **25**  
**Alternative Approach:** If the question meant "sells at marked price" instead of "sells at CP", the net profit multiplier would be $1.25 \times \frac{1000}{800} = 1.5625 \implies 56.25\%$. However, following the literal text "sells at cost price", the markup is cancelled out.  
**What makes it hard:** Detecting literal trick word clauses that render some parameters redundant.
</details>
