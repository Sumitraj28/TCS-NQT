## 1. Easy Expected Questions

**Q1.** An item is purchased for ₹250 and sold at a profit of $12\%$. Find the Selling Price.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Basic Selling Price formula: $SP = CP \times (1 + \frac{P}{100})$.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Identify variables: $CP = 250, P = 12$.
2. Calculate multiplier: $MF = 1.12$.
3. Compute:
   $$SP = 250 \times 1.12 = 280$$

**Answer:** **280**

**Shortcut:**
$$10\% \text{ of } 250 = 25, \quad 2\% \text{ of } 250 = 5 \implies \text{Profit} = 30$$
$$SP = 250 + 30 = 280$$

**Common Mistake:** Subtracting the profit from CP, leading to ₹220.

**Difficulty:** ⭐☆☆☆☆ | **Target Time:** 30 seconds

</details>

**Q2.** An article marked at ₹600 is sold for ₹480. Find the discount percentage.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Discount percentage formula: $\frac{MP - SP}{MP} \times 100$.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Identify variables: $MP = 600, SP = 480$.
2. Calculate discount value: $600 - 480 = 120$.
3. Compute:
   $$\text{Discount}\% = \frac{120}{600} \times 100 = 20\%$$

**Answer:** **20**

**Shortcut:**
$$\text{Ratio } \frac{SP}{MP} = \frac{480}{600} = \frac{4}{5} \implies \frac{1}{5} \text{ reduction} = 20\%$$

**Common Mistake:** Dividing by SP in the denominator: $\frac{120}{480} \times 100 = 25\%$.

**Difficulty:** ⭐☆☆☆☆ | **Target Time:** 30 seconds

</details>

**Q3.** Under a clearance offer, a store advertises "Buy 2, get 1 free". What is the single discount rate?
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Bundle discount formula: $\frac{\text{Free Items}}{\text{Total Items}} \times 100$.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Identify variables: Free $Y = 1$, Paid $X = 2$.
2. Total items: $X + Y = 3$.
3. Compute:
   $$\text{Discount}\% = \frac{1}{3} \times 100 = 33.33\%$$

**Answer:** **33.33**

**Shortcut:** $1$ out of $3$ items is free $= 33.33\%$.

**Common Mistake:** Using $\frac{1}{2} = 50\%$.

**Difficulty:** ⭐☆☆☆☆ | **Target Time:** 20 seconds

</details>

**Step-by-step:**

3. Compute:

**Answer:** **15**

**Shortcut:**
$$\frac{360}{2400} = \frac{36}{240} = \frac{3}{20} \implies \frac{3}{20} \times 100 = 15\%$$

**Difficulty:** ⭐☆☆☆☆ | **Target Time:** 30 seconds

**Q5.** By selling an item for ₹575, a shopkeeper makes a profit of $15\%$. Find the Cost Price.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Cost Price division formula: $CP = \frac{SP}{1 + P/100}$.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Identify variables: $SP = 575, P = 15$.
2. Set up equation:
   $$CP = \frac{575}{1.15}$$
3. Calculate:
   $$CP = 500$$

**Answer:** **500**

**Shortcut:**
$$1.15 \times CP = 575 \implies 115 \times CP = 57500 \implies CP = 500$$

**Difficulty:** ⭐☆☆☆☆ | **Target Time:** 30 seconds

</details>

## 2. Medium Expected Questions

**Q6.** A manufacturer marks his products $40\%$ above the manufacturing cost and then sells them at a discount of $20\%$. Find his net profit percentage.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Successive change: $m - d - \frac{md}{100}\%$.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Identify variables: $m = 40, d = 20$.
2. Calculate:
   $$\text{Net} = 40 - 20 - \frac{40 \times 20}{100} = 20 - 8 = 12\%$$

**Answer:** **12**

**Shortcut:** $40 - 20 - 8 = 12\%$.

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆ | **Target Time:** 30 seconds

</details>

**Q7.** If the Cost Price of 25 books is equal to the Selling Price of 20 books, find the profit percentage.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Article count equating ratio.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Set up equation: $25 \times CP = 20 \times SP$.
2. Form ratio:
   $$\frac{SP}{CP} = \frac{25}{20} = \frac{5}{4}$$
3. Profit percentage:
   $$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{5-4}{4} \times 100 = 25\%$$

**Answer:** **25**

**Shortcut:** $\frac{\text{Difference}}{\text{SP Count}} \times 100 = \frac{5}{20} \times 100 = 25\%$.

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆ | **Target Time:** 30 seconds

</details>

**Q8.** A dishonest dealer sells his flour at Cost Price but cheats on weight by delivering 950 grams instead of 1 kilogram. Find his profit percentage.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Faulty weight ratio: $\frac{\text{Error}}{\text{Delivered}} \times 100\%$.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Error weight: $1000 - 950 = 50\text{g}$.
2. Delivered weight: $950\text{g}$.
3. Compute:
   $$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{50}{950} \times 100 = \frac{1}{19} \times 100 = 5.26\%$$

**Answer:** **5.26**

**Shortcut:** $\frac{50}{950} = \frac{1}{19} \approx 5.26\%$.

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆ | **Target Time:** 45 seconds

</details>

**Q9.** A sells a watch to B at $15\%$ profit. B sells it to C at $10\%$ profit. If C pays ₹5,060, find the amount paid by A.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Multiplying factor chain.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Write equation: $CP_A \times 1.15 \times 1.10 = 5060$.
2. Combine multipliers: $1.15 \times 1.1 = 1.265$.
3. Compute CP:
   $$CP_A = \frac{5060}{1.265} = 4000$$

**Answer:** **4000**

**Shortcut:**
$$CP_A \times \frac{23}{20} \times \frac{11}{10} = 5060 \implies CP_A \times \frac{253}{200} = 5060 \implies CP_A = 20 \times 200 = 4000$$

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐☆☆☆ | **Target Time:** 50 seconds

</details>

**Q10.** Two objects are sold for ₹1,200 each. The seller makes a profit of $20\%$ on the first and a loss of $20\%$ on the second. Find the net profit or loss value in rupees.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Individual CP calculation.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Find $CP_1$:
   $$CP_1 = \frac{1200}{1.20} = 1000$$
2. Find $CP_2$:
   $$CP_2 = \frac{1200}{0.80} = 1500$$
3. Total CP $= 1000 + 1500 = 2500$.
4. Total SP $= 1200 \times 2 = 2400$.
5. Loss value:
   $$2500 - 2400 = ₹100\text{ Loss}$$

**Answer:** **100**

**Shortcut:**
$$\text{Net Loss}\% = \left(\frac{20}{10}\right)^2\% = 4\%$$
$$\text{Total CP} = \frac{\text{Total SP}}{0.96} = \frac{2400}{0.96} = 2500 \implies \text{Loss} = 100$$

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐☆☆ | **Target Time:** 60 seconds

</details>

## 3. Hard Expected Questions

**Q11.** A merchant marks up his goods by $20\%$ and gives a discount of $10\%$. Additionally, he cheats on weight by $10\%$ when selling. Find his net profit percentage.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Multi-factor profit multiplication.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Pricing factor: Markup $20\%$ and discount $10\%$.
   $$\text{Pricing SP factor} = 1.20 \times 0.90 = 1.08$$
2. Weight factor: Cheats by $10\% \implies$ gives 900g instead of 1000g.
   $$\text{Weight SP factor} = \frac{1000}{900} = \frac{10}{9}$$
3. Combine factors:
   $$MF_{\text{net}} = 1.08 \times \frac{10}{9} = 1.20$$
4. Convert to profit%: $1.20 \implies 20\%$.

**Answer:** **20**

**Shortcut:**
$$\text{Pricing Net} = 20 - 10 - 2 = 8\% \implies \text{SP for 900g} = 108$$
$$\text{SP for 1000g} = 108 \times \frac{10}{9} = 120 \implies 20\% \text{ Profit}$$

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ | **Target Time:** 60 seconds

</details>

**Q12.** An article is sold at a profit of $20\%$. If he had bought it at $20\%$ less and sold it for ₹75 less, he would have made a profit of $25\%$. Find the original Cost Price.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Hypothetical transaction equations.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Set up actual: $CP_1 = 100x \implies SP_1 = 120x$.
2. Set up hypothetical:
   *   $CP_2 = 80x$ ($20\%$ less).
   *   $SP_2 = 120x - 75$ (₹75 less).
3. Apply profit constraint: Profit $= 25\%$.
   $$SP_2 = CP_2 \times 1.25$$
   $$120x - 75 = 80x \times 1.25$$
   $$120x - 75 = 100x$$
4. Solve for $x$:
   $$20x = 75 \implies x = 3.75$$
5. Find original CP:
   $$CP_1 = 100x = 100(3.75) = ₹375$$

**Answer:** **375**

**Shortcut:**
$$120\%CP - 75 = 80\%CP \times 1.25 = 100\%CP \implies 20\%CP = 75 \implies CP = 375$$

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ | **Target Time:** 60 seconds

</details>

**Q13.** A trader marks up his goods such that even after giving a discount of $20\%$, he makes a profit of $12\%$. Find the markup percentage.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Connect MP/CP ratios using SP.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Write the Selling Price equations:
   $$SP = CP \times (1 + \frac{P}{100}) = MP \times (1 - \frac{D}{100})$$
   $$CP \times 1.12 = MP \times 0.80$$
2. Form the ratio of Marked Price to Cost Price:
   $$\frac{MP}{CP} = \frac{1.12}{0.80} = \frac{112}{80} = \frac{7}{5} = 1.40$$
3. Find the markup percentage:
   $$\text{Markup}\% = (1.40 - 1) \times 100 = 40\%$$

**Answer:** **40**

**Shortcut:**
$$\frac{MP}{CP} = \frac{100 + P}{100 - D} = \frac{112}{80} = 1.40 \implies 40\% \text{ Markup}$$

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ | **Target Time:** 50 seconds

</details>

**Q14.** A dealer sells $\frac{3}{4}\text{th}$ of his goods at a profit of $20\%$ and the remaining goods at Cost Price. Find his net profit percentage.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Weighted average profit.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Assume total goods $= 4$ units, cost $= ₹100$ per unit. Total CP $= 400$.
2. 3 units sold at $20\%$ profit $\implies$ sold for $3 \times 120 = 360$.
3. 1 unit sold at cost $\implies$ sold for 100.
4. Total SP $= 360 + 100 = 460$.
5. Net profit:
   $$\text{Profit}\% = \frac{460 - 400}{400} \times 100 = \frac{60}{400} \times 100 = 15\%$$

**Answer:** **15**

**Shortcut:**
$$\text{Net } P\% = \left(\frac{3}{4} \times 20\right) + \left(\frac{1}{4} \times 0\right) = 15\%$$

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐☆☆ | **Target Time:** 45 seconds

</details>

**Q15.** A merchant bought two cycles for a total of ₹1,600. If he sells the first cycle at $10\%$ profit and the second at $20\%$ profit, he earns a certain profit. If he sells the first at $20\%$ profit and the second at $10\%$ profit, he earns ₹10 more. Find the difference between the Cost Prices of the two cycles.
<details>
<summary>✅ Solution</summary>

**Approach:** Set up equations for two profit lines and subtract.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Let cost prices of cycles be $x$ and $y$.
   $$x + y = 1600$$
2. Profit in Case 1:
   $$P_1 = 0.10x + 0.20y$$
3. Profit in Case 2:
   $$P_2 = 0.20x + 0.10y$$
4. The difference in profit is ₹10 ($P_2 - P_1 = 10$):
   $$(0.20x + 0.10y) - (0.10x + 0.20y) = 10$$
   $$0.10x - 0.10y = 10$$
5. Multiply by 10 to clear decimal:
   $$x - y = 100$$
   The difference between the Cost Prices is ₹100.

**Answer:** **100**

**Shortcut:**
$$\Delta P = (\Delta \text{Rate}) \times (x - y) \implies 10 = 0.10 \times (x-y) \implies (x-y) = 100$$

**Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | **Target Time:** 75 seconds

</details>
