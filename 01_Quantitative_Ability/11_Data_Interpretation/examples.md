## Dataset 1 — Monthly Household Expenses (Pie-Chart represented as Table)

The following table represents the percentage distribution of the monthly household expenses of a family. 
*   **Total Monthly Income:** $\$80,000$
*   The family spends their entire monthly income across these categories.

| Expense Category | Percentage Share (%) |
| :--- | :--- |
| Rent & Housing | $25\%$ |
| Food & Groceries | $20\%$ |
| Education | $15\%$ |
| Savings | $15\%$ |
| Transport | $10\%$ |
| Miscellaneous | $15\%$ |

---

### Example 1
**Question:** What is the total amount spent on Rent & Housing and Education combined?
*   **Step-by-step Solution:**
    1.  Find the combined percentage share: $\text{Rent\%} + \text{Education\%} = 25\% + 15\% = 40\%$.
    2.  Calculate this share of the total monthly income:
        $$\text{Amount} = 40\% \text{ of } \$80,000 = \frac{40}{100} \times 80,000 = \$32,000$$
*   **Answer:** $\$32,000$

---

### Example 2
**Question:** What is the ratio of expenditure on Food & Groceries to Transport?
*   **Step-by-step Solution:**
    1.  Since the base value ($\$80,000$) is the same for both, we can directly compare their percentage shares without calculating the actual amounts:
        $$\text{Ratio} = \frac{\text{Food\%}}{\text{Transport\%}} = \frac{20\%}{10%20\%} = \frac{20}{10} = 2 : 1$$
*   **Answer:** $2 : 1$

---

### Example 3
**Question:** What is the central angle in degrees corresponding to the Miscellaneous category if represented in a pie chart?
*   **Step-by-step Solution:**
    1.  A full circle represents $360^\circ$ or $100\%$.
    2.  The conversion factor is $3.6^\circ$ per $1\%$.
    3.  For Miscellaneous ($15\%$):
        $$\text{Angle} = 15 \times 3.6^\circ = 54^\circ$$
*   **Answer:** $54^\circ$

---

### Example 4
**Question:** If the family decides to increase their Savings percentage by $5\text{ percentage points}$ next month by reducing Rent & Housing expenditure, what will be the percentage decrease in Rent & Housing expenditure?
*   **Step-by-step Solution:**
    1.  Rent & Housing share decreases from $25\%$ to $20\%$ (a $5\text{ percentage point}$ reduction).
    2.  Calculate the relative percentage decrease in Rent & Housing:
        $$\text{Percentage Decrease} = \frac{25\% - 20\%}{25\%} \times 100\% = \frac{5}{25} \times 100\% = 20\%$$
*   **Answer:** $20\%$

## Dataset 2 — Startup Financial Performance (Data Table)

The table below shows the financial performance indicators of a technology startup from 2021 to 2025 (all values are in million USD).

| Year | Total Sales ($M) | Operating Expenditure ($M) | Marketing Expenditure ($M) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 2021 | $120$ | $80$ | $20$ |
| 2022 | $150$ | $95$ | $25$ |
| 2023 | $180$ | $110$ | $35$ |
| 2024 | $220$ | $130$ | $45$ |
| 2025 | $300$ | $170$ | $60$ |

---

### Example 5
**Question:** What is the percentage increase in Total Sales from 2021 to 2025?
*   **Step-by-step Solution:**
    1.  Identify values: $\text{Sales}_{2021} = 120$, $\text{Sales}_{2025} = 300$.
    2.  Calculate percentage increase:
        $$\text{\% Increase} = \frac{300 - 120}{120} \times 100\% = \frac{180}{120} \times 100\% = 150\%$$
*   **Answer:** $150\%$

---

### Example 6
**Question:** In which year was the ratio of Marketing Expenditure to Operating Expenditure the highest?
*   **Step-by-step Solution:**
    1.  Write down ratios ($\frac{\text{Marketing}}{\text{Operating}}$) for each year:
        *   2021: $\frac{20}{80} = 0.250$
        *   2022: $\frac{25}{95} \approx 0.263$
        *   2023: $\frac{35}{110} \approx 0.318$
        *   2024: $\frac{45}{130} \approx 0.346$
        *   2025: $\frac{60}{170} \approx 0.353$
    2.  Comparing values shows that $0.353$ in 2025 is the highest.
*   **Answer:** $2025$

---

### Example 7
**Question:** What is the average annual profit (Profit = Sales - Operating Expenditure - Marketing Expenditure) of the startup over the five-year period?
*   **Step-by-step Solution:**
    1.  Calculate profits for each year:
        *   2021: $120 - 80 - 20 = 20$
        *   2022: $150 - 95 - 25 = 30$
        *   2023: $180 - 110 - 35 = 35$
        *   2024: $220 - 130 - 45 = 45$
        *   2025: $300 - 170 - 60 = 70$
    2.  Sum the profits: $20 + 30 + 35 + 45 + 70 = 200$.
    3.  Divide by number of years: $\frac{200}{5} = 40$.
*   **Answer:** $\$40\text{ million}$

---

### Example 8
**Question:** If the growth rate of Total Sales from 2025 to 2026 is projected to be the same as the growth rate from 2024 to 2025, what will be the projected sales for 2026?
*   **Step-by-step Solution:**
    1.  Calculate the growth rate from 2024 to 2025:
        $$\text{Growth Rate} = \frac{300 - 220}{220} = \frac{80}{220} = \frac{4}{11} \approx 36.36\%$$
    2.  Apply this growth rate to the 2025 sales to project 2026 sales:
        $$\text{Projected Sales}_{2026} = 300 \times \left(1 + \frac{4}{11}\right) = 300 \times \frac{15}{11} = \frac{4500}{11} \approx 409.09$$
*   **Answer:** $\$409.09\text{ million}$
