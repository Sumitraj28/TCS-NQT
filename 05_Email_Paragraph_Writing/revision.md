# Email & Paragraph Writing — Revision Guide

---

## 1. Quick Email Cheat Block

A standard professional email is composed of 7 logical parts:

```text
1. Subject Line:      Action-oriented & specific (e.g. Leave Request: [Date])
2. Salutation:        Dear [Mr./Ms. Last Name / Name],
3. Purpose:           I am writing to request/query/inform you about...
4. Details:           Provide 2-3 sentences of context or bullet points.
5. Call to Action:    Could you please review/confirm/approve this?
6. Closing:           Thank you for your time/assistance.
7. Regards & Name:    Sincerely, [Name]
```

### Tone Rules
*   **No Slang:** Avoid words like *y'all, kind of, dynamic duo, cool, ASAP* (use *as soon as possible* instead).
*   **No Contractions:** In highly formal emails, prefer *do not* over *don't*, *cannot* over *can't*.
*   **Active Voice:** Use active structures for clear responsibility (e.g., "I processed the invoice" rather than "The invoice was processed by me").

## 2. Quick Paragraph Cheat Block

Every formal paragraph must cover 4 segments:

```text
1. Introduction:     Topic sentence stating the main argument.
2. Explanation:      2-3 sentences detailing the logic and context.
3. Example:          A real-world fact, study, or statistic.
4. Conclusion:       Closing sentence wrapping up the argument.
```

### Transition Words Reference Table

| Transition Type | Function | Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Addition** | Introduce supplementary details | Furthermore, Additionally, Moreover |
| **Contrast** | Highlight opposing views or limitations | However, On the contrary, Nevertheless |
| **Cause-Effect** | Show consequences of arguments | Consequently, Therefore, As a result |
| **Illustration** | Present concrete examples | For instance, Specifically, To illustrate |
