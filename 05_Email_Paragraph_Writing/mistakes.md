# Email & Paragraph Writing — Common Mistakes

---

## 1. Top 5 Email Mistakes

Avoid these frequent errors in business correspondence:

1.  **Informal Salutations & Sign-offs:**
    *   *Mistake:* Using "Hey John," or signing off with "Thanks" or "Cheers."
    *   *Correction:* Use "Dear Mr. Smith," or "Dear John," and sign off with "Sincerely," or "Best regards,".
2.  **Punctuation Errors in Greetings:**
    *   *Mistake:* Placing a semicolon or no punctuation after the greeting (e.g., *Dear Sir;* or *Dear Mr. Brown*).
    *   *Correction:* Always place a **comma** after the greeting and the closing sign-off (e.g., *Dear Sir,* and *Sincerely,*).
3.  **Vague Subject Lines:**
    *   *Mistake:* Subject: "Request" or "Urgent."
    *   *Correction:* Make it specific and include relevant metadata: "Meeting Reschedule Request: June 28 Project Sync."
4.  **Excessive Use of Contractions:**
    *   *Mistake:* "I'm writing because I don't think we can make it."
    *   *Correction:* "I am writing because I do not believe we can meet the deadline."
5.  **Dangling Call to Action:**
    *   *Mistake:* Ending with "Let me know" or leaving the email without a clear request.
    *   *Correction:* "Please confirm if you can attend the meeting by 4:00 PM today."

## 2. Top 5 Paragraph Mistakes

Avoid these structural errors in paragraph writing:

1.  **Lack of a Topic Sentence:**
    *   *Mistake:* Starting the paragraph with an example or a random detail rather than introducing the main theme.
    *   *Correction:* Begin with a clear, general introductory sentence that summarizes the paragraph's message.
2.  **Over-complication (CAT-level vocabulary):**
    *   *Mistake:* Cramming the paragraph with obscure words that distract from the argument.
    *   *Correction:* Use clear, precise, and professional vocabulary. Cohesion is more important than showing off vocabulary.
3.  **Abrupt Transitions:**
    *   *Mistake:* Jumping from explanation to example without using transitional phrases like "For instance" or "As a result."
    *   *Correction:* Use cohesive linkers to guide the reader through your logic.
4.  **No Concluding Thought:**
    *   *Mistake:* Ending the paragraph immediately after stating the example.
    *   *Correction:* Write a concluding sentence that summarizes the main takeaway and ties the example back to the topic.
5.  **Mixing Multiple Topics:**
    *   *Mistake:* Discussing Artificial Intelligence, and then shifting midway to discuss climate change policy or remote work without a clear link.
    *   *Correction:* Keep the focus strictly on the single topic introduced in the first sentence.
